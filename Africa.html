About Botswana

Botswana is country in Southern Africa, neighboring South Africa, Namibia, and Zimbabwe. Botwana is one of the most democratic and least corrupt countries in Africa. This country is mostly democratic and has positive, friendly relations with countries all over the world. They are also an active and important participant in the African Union. As a former British colony, Botswana has more Western influence, as it is mostly a democracy and has great relations with the United States, United Kingdom, and European Union. However, they do not turn backs to the East. Botswana is also very friendly and communicative with China for economic activities. Botswana is also a leader in Southern Africa, aiming to promote stability and economic growth all over the region. This country tries their best to advocate for democracy, and looks favorably on condoemning or expressing concern about human-rights abuses and corruption in Africa. Botswana is open to discussing with any country to address these global issues.

About Egypt

Egypt is a North African country located around the Sinai Penninsula. Egypt is the most powerful and influential country in Africa. Although the country is an Authoritarian Regime, as if it aligns more to the East, Egypt seems to stand very neutral, as they have strong ties on both the East and West side. They recieve economic development aid and military relations with many different countries from across the world, especially from the United States, China, Russia, Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, France, and the rest of the European Union. Egypt also has good ties with Jordan, since the two countries have very similar positions on the conflict in Israel-Palestine. Egypt has had complex relations with Israel due to historical and recent conflicts near the Egyptian border. However, Egypt's diplomatic tensions mostly lie with Ethiopia over economic matters.

About South Africa

South Africa is the southernmost country in Africa, neighboring Botswana, Zimababwe, Namibia and others. This country is amongst the most influential countries in Africa. South Africa is former British Colony and is known to have more British influence than any other African nation. As a result, South Africa is mostly a democracy, and their politics align with the West overall. In both the African Union and United Nations, South Africa's advocacy main revolve around promoting peace and maintaining friendly diplomacy. Towards resolutions, the country pays very close attention to any potential impacts of peace in a situation, and will hesitate if there is a risk of straining relations between other countries. On the topic of relations, South Africa collaborates with nations from all around the globe. The country has stellar relations with Kenya, as the 2 nations often work together in all kinds of ways in the African Union. South Africa has friendly relations with their fellow members in BRICS: Brazil, Russia, China, and India. Furthermore, the European Union is key business partner of South Africa. Given that they do not have a military alliance, South Africa does not have completely turn their backs to other nations.
