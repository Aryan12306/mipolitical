About Canada

Canada is largest country in North America and the 2nd largest in the world. This country overall stays pretty relevant in discussing global issues in the U.N, as they have a great amount of racial diversity. Canada is one of the most democratic countries in the world. Their political stance definitely leans more towards the West. They are not just one of the founders of NATO, but the United Nations itself. Canada is a very strong friend and ally to the other nations who are part of the Western alliance. The United States and Canada have one of the best relations in the world, as they have many treaties, deals policies, and partnerships between them. The country also has a good connection with the United Kingdom and France. That being said, Canada does favor peaceful approaches to global situations. In other words, they are not afraid to abstain on Western-suggested efforts in the U.N, and their Western allies respect that. Outside of the West, Canada has stellar relations with India. The two countries often economically work together, and it is plausible to say that India is Canada's best asian ally. Canada does not exactly have any outstanding enemies, but has taken Russia as a threat. However, in the U.N, Canada does not find it beneficial to engage with the West's opposition. As a result, they will abstain to the East as well, but will communicate with the U.S about it.

This country has been looked at as suffering from "democratic backsliding", but has closer allies in the West, than they do in the East. The country mostly works with Mexico and Honduras, who also like to stand neutral.

About Nicaragua

Nicaragua is country located in the Central-American region of North America. Nicaragua has a unique stance and status compared to that of other countries in Central America. In the peak of the Cold War, Marxism-Leninism was hevaily influenced into the country's politics, which led a Sandimismo (Communisty) party to take control of the government. The country was led by a Communist regime until the end of the Cold War. As of now, Nicaragua is not officially Communist anymore. However. The country is still ruled by an Authoritarian regime with Communist-ideals. Due to this, Nicaragua politically leans well to the East and opposes the West. The country has good connections with Venezuela and Cuba, as they have close proximity to Nicaragua and have similar political history. Since the Soviet Union gave their diplomatic and economic support to the Sandinismo-support party that overthrew the government, Nicaragua has great ties with Russia. Additionally, Nicaragua follows the One-China policy and opposes the West, which strengthens their relations with China and Iran. The United States is Nicaragua's biggest enemy, as the U.S is in opposition with Nicaragua's political status, and attempted to stop the Regime from taking over. Nicaragua also has territorial conflicts with Colombia and Costa Rica.
