About Brazil

Brazil is the largest country in South America, and 5th largest in the world. They have especially strong ties with the West, as they are mostly a democracy, but may have an independent position from the West. This country is significant to U.N, not just because they are one of the strongest, most powerful nations, but also because they are one of the founders of the U.N itself. Brazil will almost always express concern on global issues, and is very passionate about helping the world address them. They are not afraid to share their voice. Brazil is a very friendly country, with great relations all over the world. Brazil has fantastic relations with India, Germany, and Japan, as all 4 countries make up the G4, a group where all countries will a support one another to have permanent seats in the U.N Security Council. China and Brazil also have great relations, given then China is Brazil's biggest trading partner. Both countries are also in the BRICS economic alliance, which is also why Brazil has friendly relations with Russia, and South Africa. Brazil does not exactly have any outstanding enemies but has strained relations with Venezuela because of political differences, As of recently, Brazil seems to have changed its diplomatic status with Israel, as the Brazilian government outwardly criticized the Israeli government's actions in the Gaza Strip. Generally, however, Brazil smiles to the rest of the world.

About Colombia

Colombia is a South American nation, bordering Panama, Venezuela, Brazil, Ecuador, and Peru. This country is very important to the Americas, as it connects the North and South with Panama. Colombia is also significant to the world because it is a founding member of the United Nations itself. The country was part of the Spanish Empire until 1819. Now, they are mostly democratic, and their political stace aligns well with the West. In the U.N, Colombia prioritizes equal human rights, drug policy, and regional stability. Something Colombia is known for in their civilization is human-rights protection, espeically for local Women, Children and indigenous groups. Issues with drug policy has put Colombia's stability at risk, similar to their neighbors. As a result, Colombia works to address drug policy reform to create a healthier public environment for their country and their neighbors. On this topic, Colombia is supported by most of South America, especially Brazil. The two nations have often worked together to promote regional stability. Overall, the United States has been Colombia's strongest ally. The U.S has historically provided military assistance to them. However, Colombia has has some tensions other countries due to border security. Panama and Colombia have often dealt with illegal migration and drug trafficking, causing a few arguments. Tensions with Ecuador have followed the same reasons. Additionally, Venezuela and Colombia have had quite a few disagreements in politics and border disputes.
